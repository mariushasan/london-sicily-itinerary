## MUST-INCLUDE ATTRACTIONS

---

### 1. Fontana dell'Elefante (u Liotru)
- **Location:** Piazza del Duomo (center of the square)
- **Hours:** 24/7 - outdoor public fountain
- **Cost:** Free
- **Duration:** 10-15 min
- **Google Maps:** [Fontana dell'Elefante](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Fontana+dell'Elefante+Catania)
- **Notes:** The symbol of Catania - a black basalt elephant statue from the Roman era carrying an Egyptian obelisk. Designed by Vaccarini (1735-1737). Best photographed in the morning light. Right in the center of Piazza del Duomo, so you'll see it when visiting the Cathedral.

---

### 2. Basilica Cattedrale di Sant'Agata (Catania Cathedral / Duomo)
- **Location:** Piazza Duomo, 10 - 95124 Catania
- **Hours (Friday):**
  - Morning: 7:00 AM - 12:00 PM
  - Afternoon: 4:00 PM - 7:00 PM
  - **MIDDAY CLOSURE: 12:00 PM - 4:00 PM**
- **Cost:** FREE (main cathedral)
  - Combined ticket (Cathedral + Achilliane Thermae underground Roman baths + Diocesan Museum): EUR 12
- **Duration:** 30-45 min (cathedral only), 1-1.5 hours (with baths & museum)
- **Google Maps:** [Cattedrale Sant'Agata](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Basilica+Cattedrale+Sant'Agata+Catania)
- **Phone:** 095 320044
- **Tips:**
  - The Achilliane Thermae (Roman baths) are accessed through a door on the right facade - worth seeing
  - Contains the tomb of composer Vincenzo Bellini
  - Shoulders and knees must be covered
  - Artworks inside depict the 1669 Mount Etna eruption
  - Facade features characteristic contrast between gray lava stone and white marble

### 3. La Pescheria (Fish Market)
- **Location:** Behind/below Piazza del Duomo (accessed via steps behind the fountain)
- **Hours (Friday):** 7:00 AM - 2:00 PM
  - **BEST TIME: 7:00 AM - 10:00 AM** (most lively, best selection)
  - Stalls start closing before noon
- **Cost:** Free to walk through
- **Duration:** 30-60 min (depending on how much you browse/eat)
- **Google Maps:** [La Pescheria Catania](https://www.google.com/maps/search/La+Pescheria+fish+market+Catania)
- **Tips:**
  - This is the soul of Catania - a sensory overload of fish, colors, shouting vendors
  - Look for the oyster vendor who opens them fresh with lemon and white wine
  - Street food stalls around the market serve fried seafood cones (cartoccio di mare)
  - Closed on Sundays and public holidays
  - Wednesday closes at 1PM (not relevant for your Friday)
  - Wear shoes you don't mind getting wet - the ground is often sloshed with water and fish debris

### 4. Teatro Antico Greco-Romano di Catania (Roman Theatre & Odeon)
- **Location:** Via Vittorio Emanuele II, 266 - 95124 Catania
- **Hours (Friday - April/Summer schedule):** 9:00 AM - 7:00 PM daily
  - **Last entry:** 6:30 PM
- **Cost:**
  - Full: EUR 7-8
  - Reduced: EUR 3.50-4
  - Free first Sunday of the month
- **Duration:** 45 min - 1 hour
- **Google Maps:** [Teatro Antico Catania](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Teatro+Antico+Romano+Catania)
- **Phone:** +39 095 7150508
- **Tips:**
  - Includes both the large Roman Theatre (7,000-seat capacity, built of black lava rock) and the smaller adjacent Odeon
  - The Odeon is directly connected west of the theatre and sits slightly higher
  - Surrounded by residential buildings from later centuries - a fascinating urban archaeology site
  - Gladiator tunnels are accessible underground
  - About a 5-min walk from Piazza Duomo

### 5. Porta Garibaldi
- **Location:** End of Via Giuseppe Garibaldi, between Piazza Palestro and Piazza Crocifisso
- **Hours:** 24/7 - outdoor public monument
- **Cost:** Free
- **Duration:** 10-15 min
- **Google Maps:** [Porta Garibaldi Catania](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Porta+Garibaldi+Catania)
- **Notes:**
  - Triumphal arch built in 1768 to celebrate the marriage of Ferdinand I to Maria Carolina of Austria
  - Made of alternating white limestone from Siracusa and black lava blocks - very photogenic
  - Features a clock at the top, an eagle, and an elephant statue
  - Designed by architects Battaglia and Ittar
  - Located at the eastern end of Via Garibaldi, which connects back to Piazza Duomo (~10 min walk)

---

### 6. Centro Storico (Historic Center Streets)
- **Key streets:** Via Etnea, Via Vittorio Emanuele, Via Garibaldi, Piazza Universita
- **Hours:** Always accessible
- **Cost:** Free
- **Duration:** Woven throughout the day's walking
- **Tips:**
  - Via Etnea is the main north-south axis - on clear days you can see Mount Etna perfectly framed at the end of the street
  - Via Vittorio Emanuele is the main east-west axis connecting major sites
  - Piazza Universita is a beautiful baroque piazza with ornate lamp posts, ~2 min walk up Via Etnea from Piazza Duomo
  - Most of the centro storico is pedestrian-friendly and walkable
  - The black lava-stone paving is distinctive to Catania

  - Located about a 10-min walk west of Piazza Duomo

### 8. Chiesa della Badia di Sant'Agata (Rooftop Views) -- HIGHLY RECOMMENDED
- **Location:** Via Vittorio Emanuele, 182 (directly opposite the Cathedral)
- **Hours (Friday):**
  - Morning: 9:30 AM - 12:30 PM
  - Afternoon: 6:00 PM - 9:00 PM (Fri & Sat spring/summer hours)
- **Cost:**
  - Church: Free
  - Dome & terrace tour: EUR 3
  - Combined (dome + terrace + Diocesan Museum + Achilliane Thermae): EUR 12
- **Duration:** 20-30 min
- **Google Maps:** [Badia Sant'Agata Catania](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Chiesa+Badia+Sant'Agata+Catania)
- **Phone:** 327 1352519 / 348 7967711
- **Tips:**
  - BEST PANORAMIC VIEW IN CATANIA - see the cathedral dome, the port, Mount Etna, and the baroque rooftops
  - 170 steps to the top (elevator available to the terrace level, then 57 more steps)
  - Only EUR 3 for the terrace - possibly the best value viewpoint in Sicily
  - Right next to the cathedral and Piazza Duomo - easy to combine
  - Go in the morning for the best light on Etna

### 9. Via Crociferi (Baroque Street) -- HIGHLY RECOMMENDED
- **Location:** Starts at Piazza San Francesco d'Assisi, enters under Arco di San Benedetto
- **Hours:** Always accessible (street is public); individual churches have varying hours
- **Cost:** Free (street walking)
- **Duration:** 20-30 min walk
- **Google Maps:** [Via Crociferi Catania](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Via+Crociferi+Catania)
- **Tips:**
  - UNESCO World Heritage street - only ~400 meters long but packed with Baroque masterpieces
  - Four major churches along the street plus the Jesuit College and Villa Cerami
  - The Arco di San Benedetto at the entrance is iconic - built in 1704 to connect two parts of the monastery
  - Early morning or late afternoon best for photos and avoiding crowds
  - Used as a filming location for several Italian movies
  - Can easily be combined with visiting Teatro Antico (adjacent area)

---

### 10. Arco di San Benedetto -- INCLUDE (part of Via Crociferi walk)
- **Location:** Beginning of Via Crociferi at Piazza San Francesco d'Assisi
- **Hours:** Always visible (outdoor arch)
- **Cost:** Free
- **Duration:** 5 min (part of Via Crociferi walk)
- **Notes:** Built in 1704 by Benedictine nuns to connect the two halves of their monastery. According to legend, built in a single night to circumvent a city ban. View from below and also from the street.

### BREAKFAST (Colazione Siciliana)

The Sicilian breakfast is granita con brioche - a slushy ice treat in various flavors served with a soft, buttery brioche with a "tuppo" (the round knob on top).

**Top breakfast spots:**

1. **Pasticceria Savia** -- Via Etnea 300/302/304
   - Hours: 8AM-9:20PM daily
   - Famous for: Arancini, granita, cannoli, cipolline
   - Price: EUR 3-5 for granita + brioche
   - Tip: Can be busy with mediocre service but great food. Ask for whipped cream on your granita.
   - Location: Right across from Villa Bellini on Via Etnea

2. **Pasticceria Spinella** -- Via Etnea (near Savia, also across from Villa Bellini)
   - Famous for: Granita, brioche, treccine, cappuccino
   - Historic establishment since 1936
   - Locals give Savia the edge for arancini but Spinella for pastries

3. **Bar Alecci**
   - Famous for: Bronte pistachio granita, lemon granita
   - Tip: "No tourists, no English, just pure local experience"
   - Great for an authentic colazione experience

4. **Bar Pasticceria Arabica**
   - Famous for: Cipollina (savory puff pastry), coffee, granita
   - Great all-around breakfast spot

**Must-try breakfast items:**
- Granita di mandorla tostata (toasted almond) con brioche
- Granita di pistacchio con brioche
- Granita di limone (classic refreshing option)
- Cipollina (savory pastry with onion, tomato, ham, mozzarella)
- Cartocciata (stuffed dough, like a mini calzone)

### LUNCH (Pranzo)

**Budget-friendly local lunch options:**

1. **Scirocco Sicilian Fish Lab** -- Piazza Alonzo di Benedetto 7 (at the fish market)
   - Hours: 10:30AM-11PM daily
   - Price: EUR 10-20 per person
   - Famous for: Fried seafood cones (cartoccio di mare, EUR 5-7), octopus salad, swordfish sandwiches
   - Tip: Located right at the Pescheria - perfect for a quick, affordable seafood lunch after visiting the market

2. **Osteria Antica Marina** -- Via Pardo 29 (inside the fish market area)
   - Hours: Lunch from ~12:30PM
   - Price: Moderate (EUR 25-40 for a full meal)
   - Famous for: Ultra-fresh seafood pasta, sea urchin dishes, scampi
   - Tip: Reservations recommended. One of the most acclaimed fish restaurants in Catania. Atmospheric location inside the market.

3. **A Putia Do' Calabrisi** -- Via della Concordia, 185
   - Price: ~EUR 10 fixed menu (starter + primo + secondo)
   - Famous for: Purely Catanese cuisine, fried triglie fish, pork rind in tomato sauce
   - Tip: "Where to eat in Catania like a REAL local." No written menu, set price, local wine in pitchers. A bit away from the center but worth it.

4. **Trattoria De Fiore** -- Via Coppola 24/26
   - Famous for: Pasta alla Norma (the Catania signature dish), homemade maccheroni, caponata, parmigiana
   - Tip: Family-run since the 1960s, with 70-year-old Mamma Rosanna still cooking daily. Authentic and affordable.

5. **Trattoria del Cavaliere** -- Via Paternò 11
   - Famous for: Pasta alla Norma, rich appetizer selection
   - Price: "Extremely fair pricing"
   - Tip: Casual Catanese style, no pretension, excellent traditional food

6. **La Vuciata** -- Via Gisira, 8 (near the fish market)
   - Famous for: Traditional recipes with innovation, Etna wines
   - Tip: Owner Marco emphasizes product quality. Good location near Piazza Duomo.

## HORSE MEAT NOTE (Via Plebiscito)

The horse meat restaurants on Via Plebiscito are primarily an **evening** experience. The street fills with charcoal grill smoke in the evening. If your itinerary is daytime only, you'll likely miss this. Key spots:
- **Achille** - Most famous
- **La Terrazza del Barone** - Also serves Sicilian pasta and seafood during the day

## PRACTICAL NOTES

- **Walking distances:** Everything in the centro storico is within a 20-min walk. The most distant site from center is Monastero dei Benedettini (~10 min from Piazza Duomo).
- **Footwear:** Wear comfortable shoes - Catania's lava-stone streets can be uneven.
- **Water:** Bring a water bottle. April can already be warm in Sicily.
- **Pickpockets:** Standard precautions in market areas.
- **Language:** Less English spoken than in Palermo. Key phrases in Italian help.
- **Easter closure:** April 24 is well after Easter (April 5, 2026) so all Easter closures will have ended.
