# Day 3 - Catania
*Area: Centro Storico*

> **Check before you go:** La Pescheria closes by early afternoon — go first thing for the full experience. Cathedral closes 12PM-4PM. Book your Monastero dei Benedettini guided tour in advance at [monasterodeibenedettini.it](https://www.monasterodeibenedettini.it/en/). Castello Ursino is closed for renovation until at least 2027.

---

### 1. [La Pescheria](https://www.google.com/maps/search/La+Pescheria+fish+market+Catania)
- **Time:** 8:00AM (the earlier the better)
- **What:** Catania's legendary open-air fish market — swordfish halves, mountains of sea urchin, octopus in buckets, vendors shouting over each other. The surrounding stalls sell fried calamari, raw oysters (~€1 each), and arancini — this is your breakfast
- **Tip:** Runs Mon-Sat, busiest 7-10AM. Wander the side alleys too — the energy spills everywhere

### 2. [Fontana dell'Elefante (u' Liotru)](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Fontana+dell'Elefante+Piazza+Duomo+Catania)
- **Time:** After the market
- **What:** Catania's symbol — a smiling lava-stone elephant carrying an Egyptian obelisk. Vaccarini designed it in 1736 as the city's good luck charm
- **Walk:** 2 min north to Piazza Duomo

### 3. [Basilica Cattedrale di Sant'Agata](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Cattedrale+Sant'Agata+Catania)
- **Time:** Before 12PM (closed 12PM-4PM)
- **What:** Built over Roman baths and dedicated to Catania's patron saint. Norman exterior, opulent Baroque interior. The chapel of Sant'Agata holds the saint's relics in a stunning silver reliquary
- **Cost:** Free (€12 combined ticket includes the Roman baths beneath — worth it)

### 4. [Chiesa della Badia di Sant'Agata (rooftop)](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Badia+di+Sant'Agata+Catania)
- **Time:** 9:30AM-12:30PM (morning session)
- **What:** The best panoramic view in Catania — cathedral dome, Etna looming behind, the port below. Only €3 and far less crowded than you'd expect
- **Walk:** Right next to the Cathedral on Via Vittorio Emanuele

### 5. [Porta Garibaldi](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Porta+Garibaldi+Catania)
- **Time:** Any time (open landmark)
- **What:** Striking 1768 triumphal arch in alternating white limestone and black lava stone — a perfect symbol of Catania's relationship with Etna
- **Walk:** ~10 min south along Via Giuseppe Garibaldi from Piazza Duomo

---

### 6. [Teatro Antico Greco-Romano](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Teatro+Antico+Romano+Catania+Via+Vittorio+Emanuele)
- **Time:** 9AM-7PM (last entry 6:30PM)
- **What:** A 2nd-century Roman theatre seating 7,000, built directly over the Greek original. The adjacent Odeon (a smaller rehearsal theatre) is included. Well-preserved and often empty
- **Walk:** ~12 min northwest from Porta Garibaldi through the centro storico backstreets
- **Cost:** €7-8

### 7. [Via Crociferi](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Via+Crociferi+Catania)
- **Time:** Any time
- **What:** UNESCO-listed Baroque street — 400 metres of ornate church facades with the Arco di San Benedetto arching overhead. One of the most beautiful streets in Sicily
- **Walk:** 3 min north from Teatro Antico

### Optional: [Chiesa di San Benedetto](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Chiesa+San+Benedetto+Via+Crociferi+Catania)
- **Time:** 10AM-1:30PM, 2:30-6:30PM
- **What:** Called Catania's "Sistine Chapel" — an explosion of frescoes covering every inch of the ceiling
- **Cost:** €6

---

### Lunch: [Scirocco Sicilian Fish Lab](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Scirocco+Sicilian+Fish+Lab+Catania)
- **Time:** ~12:30-1:30PM
- **What:** Quick, affordable fish dishes right at the edge of the Pescheria. Order whatever's freshest — the mixed fried plate and the crudo are excellent. Around €10-20
- **Walk:** ~8 min from Via Crociferi back toward the market area
- **Alternative:** [Trattoria de Fiore](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Trattoria+de+Fiore+Catania) for the definitive pasta alla Norma — Catania's signature dish: pasta with fried aubergine, ricotta salata, tomato, and basil

---

### 8. [Monastero dei Benedettini di San Nicolò l'Arena](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Monastero+dei+Benedettini+San+Nicolo+Catania)
- **Time:** Book a 2:30PM guided tour
- **What:** One of the largest Benedictine monasteries in Europe — UNESCO World Heritage. Now part of the University of Catania. Stunning cloisters, Baroque corridors, and Roman-era ruins discovered underneath
- **Walk:** ~15 min west from the market area to Piazza Dante
- **Cost:** €10
- **Tip:** Guided tour only — book online at [monasterodeibenedettini.it](https://www.monasterodeibenedettini.it/en/)

### 9. [Anfiteatro Romano](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Anfiteatro+Romano+Piazza+Stesicoro+Catania)
- **Time:** Open until 7PM
- **What:** Sunken remains of a massive Roman amphitheatre — originally larger than Verona's — sitting in the middle of Piazza Stesicoro. Only a fraction is excavated; the rest lies beneath the surrounding buildings
- **Walk:** ~10 min east from the Monastero to Via Etnea
- **Cost:** €3-4

### 10. [Villa Bellini](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Villa+Bellini+Catania)
- **Time:** Closes around sunset (~7:30PM in April)
- **What:** Catania's loveliest park — shaded paths, ornamental gardens, a hilltop bandstand with a clear view of Etna. A perfect wind-down after a full day of walking
- **Walk:** 3 min north from Piazza Stesicoro on Via Etnea

### Granita break: [Pasticceria Savia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Pasticceria+Savia+Via+Etnea+Catania)
- **What:** A Catania institution since 1897. Order a granita con brioche — almond or pistachio — the quintessential Sicilian treat. Their arancini are legendary too
- **Walk:** Right on Via Etnea, facing Villa Bellini
- **Alternative:** [Pasticceria Spinella](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Pasticceria+Spinella+Catania) across the street — locals will argue endlessly about which is better

---

### Optional: [Palazzo Biscari](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Palazzo+Biscari+Catania)
- **What:** The most lavish private palazzo in Catania — rococo ballroom, frescoed ceilings, rooftop views. Facade under restoration but interior tours may still run — check [palazzobiscari.it](https://www.palazzobiscari.it/en/guided-tours/) for availability
- **Cost:** €10
