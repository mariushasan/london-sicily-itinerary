# Day 5 - Syracuse (day trip from Catania)
*Morning: Neapolis Archaeological Park · Afternoon: Ortigia Island*

> **Getting there:** ~1 hour by train from Catania Centrale. Trains run roughly every hour.
> **Check before you go:** Buy the **combined ticket** at the Archaeological Park (Park + Palazzo Bellomo = €13, saves €8). Ortigia Market is Mon-Sat only — closed Sundays. Castello Maniace may close as early as 4:30PM. Wear sturdy shoes for the Archaeological Park (uneven rocky ground).

### Trains
- **Catania → Syracuse:** Take an early regional train (~7:00AM) from Catania Centrale. ~1 hour, ~7-10 EUR
- **Syracuse → Catania:** Return trains run until late evening. ~1 hour, ~7-10 EUR
- **Tickets:** Buy on [Trenitalia](https://www.trenitalia.com/en.html) (app or website) or at the station machines. Regional tickets don't need advance booking — validate paper tickets at the green machines on the platform!

---

### 1. [Parco Archeologico della Neapolis](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Parco+Archeologico+della+Neapolis+Siracusa)
- **Time:** 8:30AM (opens — go first to beat the crowds and midday sun)
- **What:** One of the most important archaeological sites in the Mediterranean, all on a single ticket:
  - **Teatro Greco** — a colossal theatre carved into the hillside in the 5th century BC, seating up to 15,000. One of the largest Greek theatres ever built
  - **Orecchio di Dionisio** — a 23-metre-high artificial cave with extraordinary acoustics. Legend says the tyrant Dionysius used it to eavesdrop on prisoners
  - **Latomia del Paradiso** — ancient stone quarry turned into a lush, shaded garden. Hauntingly beautiful
  - **Anfiteatro Romano** — near the entrance, partly carved from the bedrock
  - **Ara di Ierone II** — a 200-metre-long sacrificial altar, one of the largest in the ancient Greek world
- **Cost:** €13 (€6.50 reduced EU 18-25; free under 18). **Get the combined ticket with Palazzo Bellomo for €13 — saves €8**
- **Duration:** ~1.5-2 hours
- **Getting there:** ~20 min walk or quick taxi (~€8) northwest from the station
- **Tip:** Mostly outdoors and unshaded — bring water, hat, and sunscreen

### Optional: [Catacombe di San Giovanni](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Catacombe+di+San+Giovanni+Siracusa)
- **Time:** 9:30AM-12:30PM morning session (guided tours only, ~30 min)
- **What:** Early Christian catacombs beneath a roofless 6th-century basilica. The Crypt of San Marciano — Syracuse's first bishop — is the highlight
- **Walk:** ~10 min from the Archaeological Park
- **Cost:** €8

*Walk to Ortigia: ~30 min south through the city, or grab minibus 2 from Corso Gelone (€1, ~15 min)*

---

### 2. [Tempio di Apollo](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Tempio+di+Apollo+Siracusa)
- **Time:** Any time (outdoor ruins)
- **What:** The oldest Doric temple in Sicily (6th century BC) — massive stone remnants right at the gateway to Ortigia
- **Walk:** Right at the entrance to the island, on Piazza Pancali
- **Cost:** Free

### 3. [Mercato di Ortigia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Mercato+di+Ortigia+Siracusa) + [Caseificio Borderi](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Caseificio+Borderi+Siracusa)
- **Time:** Mon-Sat before 2PM (best before noon). **Closed Sundays**
- **What:** Vibrant market with fresh fish, fruit, cheeses, olives, and street food. The unmissable stop is **Caseificio Borderi** — legendary sandwiches piled impossibly high with fresh mozzarella, prosciutto, sun-dried tomatoes, and pesto. You'll sample cheeses while you queue. One of the best sandwich shops in Italy
- **Walk:** 2 min from Temple of Apollo, along Via de Benedictis
- **Tip:** Grab a cannolo filled fresh on the spot from one of the market stalls

### 4. [Piazza Duomo](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Piazza+Duomo+Siracusa)
- **Time:** Any time
- **What:** One of the most beautiful piazzas in all of Italy — a sweeping Baroque masterpiece in luminous white limestone, rebuilt after the 1693 earthquake. The Cathedral, the Municipio, and ornate palazzi surround you. Sit on the cathedral steps and take it in
- **Walk:** ~5 min south through Ortigia's narrow streets from the market

### 5. [Duomo di Siracusa](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Duomo+di+Siracusa)
- **Time:** ~9AM-5:30PM (no midday closure)
- **What:** Unlike any cathedral you've ever seen — built directly into a 5th-century BC Greek temple to Athena. The massive Doric columns are still visible, embedded in the Baroque walls. 2,500 years of continuous sacred use in one building
- **Cost:** €2
- **Tip:** Cover shoulders and knees. Look for the ancient Greek columns on the left exterior wall — they'll take your breath away

---

### Lunch: [Taverna Giudecca](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Taverna+Giudecca+Siracusa)
- **Time:** 12:30-2:30PM
- **What:** Tiny five-table enoteca tucked in the old Jewish Quarter. Outstanding antipasti platters — caponata, local cheeses, salami — paired with excellent Nero d'Avola and Grillo wines. Around €25 per person
- **Walk:** ~5 min south from Piazza Duomo into the backstreets
- **Alternative:** [Spizzuliamu](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Spizzuliamu+Ortigia+Siracusa) for fresh fish — try the trofie with swordfish, aubergine, and mint, paired with a cold Grillo

---

### 6. [Fonte Aretusa](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Fonte+Aretusa+Siracusa)
- **Time:** The overlook is free and always open. Internal walkway: 4PM-7PM Tue-Sun (closed Mon)
- **What:** A mythical freshwater spring right by the sea, thick with papyrus plants — one of only two places papyrus grows wild in Europe. Named for the nymph Arethusa, who was transformed into a spring to escape the river god Alpheus
- **Walk:** ~3 min west along the waterfront promenade from the Jewish Quarter
- **Cost:** Free (overlook) / €5 (internal walkway from 4PM)

### 7. [Castello Maniace](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Castello+Maniace+Siracusa)
- **Time:** 8:30AM-4:30PM (check for extended spring hours)
- **What:** Imposing 13th-century fortress built by Frederick II at the very tip of Ortigia. The sea stretches out on three sides — on a clear day you can see across to the mainland. Houses a small antiquarium of Greek and Roman finds
- **Walk:** ~5 min south from Fonte Aretusa to the island's tip
- **Cost:** €9 (free under 18)

### 8. [Mikveh - Bagno Ebraico](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Bagno+Ebraico+Mikveh+Siracusa)
- **Time:** Tours every 30 min, ~9AM-7PM
- **What:** The oldest known Jewish ritual bath in Europe (~6th century). Hidden for 500 years after the 1492 expulsion and rediscovered in the 1980s during hotel renovation. You descend 57 steps underground to ancient stone baths fed by natural springs. Extraordinary
- **Walk:** ~10 min back north through the Jewish Quarter to Via Alagona
- **Cost:** €5-7
- **Tip:** Enter through the Alla Giudecca hotel lobby. No photography inside. Tours last ~20-30 min

### 9. [Galleria Regionale di Palazzo Bellomo](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Palazzo+Bellomo+Siracusa)
- **Time:** Mon-Sat 9AM-6:30PM; Sun 9AM-1:30PM
- **What:** Beautiful 13th-century palace housing Antonello da Messina's exquisite *Annunciation* (1474) — a masterpiece of stillness and light, and one of Sicily's greatest paintings
- **Walk:** ~5 min north along Via Capodieci
- **Cost:** Free with the combined ticket from the Archaeological Park. Otherwise €8

### Granita break: [Voglia Matta](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Voglia+Matta+Ortigia+Siracusa)
- **What:** The locals' pick for the best granita in Ortigia — pistachio, almond, or mulberry with sweet brioche. Outstanding cannoli too
- **Walk:** On Via Maestranza, in the heart of Ortigia — a perfect stop on the walk back toward the bridge

### Optional: [Lungomare di Levante](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Lungomare+Ortigia+Siracusa)
- **What:** The eastern waterfront promenade — beautiful for a sunset stroll before heading back to the station. Views across the harbour to the mainland
